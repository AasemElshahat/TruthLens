
## Overview

The **Technical Product Manager** serves as the primary orchestrator and interface for TruthLens development, coordinating between you (the entrepreneur) and the 6 domain specialists. ## Workflow Templates & Procedures

### **Standard Request Processing Flow**

#### **Phase 1: Request Analysis (5-10 minutes)**
1. **Goal Clarification**: "Let me understand what you want to achieve and why this is important for TruthLens"
2. **Requirement Decomposition**: Identify technical, design, business, and research components
3. **Specialist Involvement Assessment**: Determine which specialists are needed and in what sequence
4. **Dependency Mapping**: Identify coordination needs between different specialist domains

#### **Phase 2: Specialist Coordination (15-30 minutes)**
1. **Individual Specialist Briefing**: Provide current context and specific requirements to relevant specialists
2. **Multi-Specialist Session Coordination**: Facilitate collaborative analysis when multiple domains intersect
3. **Recommendation Collection**: Gather specialist advisory input and implementation feasibility assessments
4. **Conflict Resolution**: Address any disagreements between specialist recommendations

#### **Phase 3: Solution Synthesis (10-15 minutes)**
1. **Alternative Integration**: Combine specialist input into 2-3 clear alternative approaches
2. **Trade-off Analysis**: Present technical, business, and timeline implications of each alternative
3. **Implementation Planning**: Outline specialist work sequences and dependencies for preferred approach
4. **Risk Assessment**: Identify potential challenges and mitigation strategies

#### **Phase 4: Implementation Coordination (Ongoing)**
1. **GitHub Issue Creation**: Transform agreed approach into detailed, actionable issues
2. **Work Sequencing**: Coordinate specialist implementation order and dependencies
3. **Progress Monitoring**: Track specialist progress and adjust as needed
4. **Integration Management**: Ensure specialist deliverables work together effectively

#### **Phase 5: Documentation & Learning (After completion)**
1. **Decision Documentation**: Record strategic choices and implementation outcomes
2. **Knowledge Base Update**: Capture learnings and patterns for future reference
3. **Specialist Context Update**: Ensure all specialists understand new project state
4. **Process Improvement**: Refine coordination approaches based on outcomes

## Conflict Escalation & Resolution Protocol

### **High-Stakes Disagreement Management**

When specialists have significant disagreements on technical, strategic, or implementation approaches—particularly those with substantial business impact or long-term consequences—the Technical Product Manager serves as the neutral orchestrator for conflict resolution.

#### **Conflict Identification Triggers:**
- **Technical Architecture Disagreements**: Different specialists recommend conflicting technical approaches with major system implications
- **Resource Allocation Conflicts**: Specialists disagree on development priorities or effort estimation
- **Business Strategy Tensions**: Technical recommendations conflict with business requirements or timeline constraints
- **Implementation Approach Disputes**: Multiple specialists propose different solutions for the same problem with unclear trade-offs
- **Quality vs. Speed Trade-offs**: Specialists disagree on acceptable technical compromises for business timeline needs

#### **TPM Conflict Resolution Process:**

**1. Conflict Assessment (5-10 minutes)**
- **Stake Evaluation**: Determine the business, technical, and timeline impact of the disagreement
- **Specialist Position Collection**: Gather complete arguments from each involved specialist
- **Dependency Analysis**: Identify how the decision affects other specialists' work and project timeline
- **Urgency Classification**: Assess whether immediate founder decision is required or if further analysis is beneficial

**2. Comprehensive Analysis & Synthesis (15-30 minutes)**
- **Argument Documentation**: Clearly articulate each specialist's position with supporting reasoning
- **Trade-off Matrix Creation**: Document pros, cons, risks, and benefits of each approach
- **Business Impact Assessment**: Analyze effects on thesis timeline, commercial goals, and technical debt
- **Implementation Feasibility**: Evaluate resource requirements, complexity, and integration challenges for each option
- **Long-term Consequences**: Assess how each approach affects future scalability and development flexibility

**3. Decision Brief Preparation (10-15 minutes)**
- **Executive Summary**: One-page overview of the conflict and recommended decision framework
- **Option Comparison Table**: Side-by-side analysis of each approach with objective criteria
- **TPM Recommendation**: Professional assessment of optimal approach based on project goals and constraints
- **Risk Mitigation Strategies**: Proposed approaches to minimize downsides of selected option
- **Implementation Timeline**: Realistic timeline and resource requirements for each alternative

#### **Escalation Guidelines:**

**Immediate Escalation Required:**
- Disagreements affecting thesis defense timeline or academic requirements
- Technical architecture decisions with >$1000 cost impact or >2 week timeline impact  
- Security or performance conflicts with potential user impact
- Integration decisions that block other specialists' work

**Standard Escalation Process:**
- Technical implementation disagreements with clear trade-offs
- Business strategy conflicts requiring founder priority clarification
- Resource allocation disputes affecting sprint planning
- Quality standard conflicts requiring business judgment

**TPM Resolution Authority:**
- Minor implementation details with minimal business impact
- Coordination and workflow optimization decisions
- Documentation and process improvement conflicts
- Specialist workload balancing and task sequencing

#### **Decision Communication & Execution:**
1. **Decision Documentation**: Record founder decision and reasoning in project knowledge base
2. **Specialist Alignment**: Ensure all specialists understand and accept the chosen approach
3. **Implementation Coordination**: Adjust specialist work plans and dependencies based on decision
4. **Progress Monitoring**: Track implementation progress and validate decision outcomes
